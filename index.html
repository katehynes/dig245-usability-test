What Is Usability?

In the web design world, usability refers to how easily users can navigate around a site. According to Jakob Nielsen in his article "Usability 101: Introduction to Usability" (linked here), usability is measured according to a website's learnability, efficiency, memorability, its users’ satisfaction, and their performance errors. It's easy to imagine why usability should be important to web designers – if people can't find the information they need on your website, they'll look somewhere else. Nielsen recommends a process called usability testing to understand how their website appears from a user's perspective. The process is simple: bring in a small representative sample of users to navigate through your design, observe their experiences, modify your design accordingly, and repeat.
