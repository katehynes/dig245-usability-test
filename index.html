DIG 245 Usability Test: North Carolina DMV

WHAT IS USABILITY?

In the web design world, usability refers to how easily users can navigate around a site. According to Jakob Nielsen in his article "Usability 101: Introduction to Usability" (linked here), usability is measured according to a website's learnability, efficiency, memorability, its users’ satisfaction, and their performance errors.

It's easy to imagine why usability should be important to web designers – if people can't find the information they need on your website, they'll look somewhere else. Nielsen recommends a process called usability testing to understand how their website appears from a user's perspective. The process is simple: bring in a small representative sample of users to navigate through your design, observe their experiences, modify your design accordingly, and repeat.

TESTING THE NCDMV WEBSITE

We asked two people to do a usability test on the North Carolina DMV website. We timed them as they performed 5 basic tasks to simulate a user’s experience with the site. Here are their results...



MAKE AN APPOINTMENT

User1: 45 sec
User2: 1 min 35 sec

Both users had to scroll significantly to find "Make an Appointment" button



FIND HOURS OF OPERATION

User1: 2 min, 35 sec
User2: 35 sec

Had to first search for a specific office location, then click on that office

REPLACE LICENSE/
DUPLICATE ID

User1: 30 sec
User2: 25 sec

FIND LICENSE SERVICES

User1: 15 sec
User2: 1 min 45 sec

SEND A MESSAGE

User1: 2 min
User2: 15 sec

KEY FINDINGS

Time to complete tasks varied pretty significantly between the two users. This was in part due to differences in pages they visited while trying to complete earlier tasks.

Information seemed to be repeated acoss several pages, which helped ensure users could access information regardless of where they searched for it, but some navigation routes appeared unneccessarily complicated.

User2 commented that the amount of information was somewhat overwhelming and “a little bit scattered,” although the overall experience was “better than expected.”




DISCUSSION, RECOMMENDATIONS

The users walked away from the task feeling neutral about, but ultimately satisfied with, their experience. Most tasks were relatively painless for the users – the longest each user spent on a task was 2 minutes. Spending two minutes on a task is acceptable because this website is an official government page and not intended to market a product or capture users’ attention.

The website’s main weakness is its overwhelming amount of information. However, because this website’s primary function is to inform users, and it has to serve an extremely wide audience, I would not recommend changing this aspect. This is better than limiting the information to one location, which would make it harder to find, and might lead to a more frustrating user experience.

We recommend that the site display an office location’s opening and closing hours when hovering over the location on the interactive map.
